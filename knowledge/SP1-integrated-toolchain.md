## Meaning

Transform ERP intelligently with unified, all-encompassing insights. The integrated toolchain connects People, Processes, Applications, and Data into an intelligent source of truth — so business and IT can align, de-risk change, and move from value identified to value realized.

LeanIX and Signavio share an integrated meta model: different domains keep their depth, but critical objects reference each other and stay aligned for transformation decisions. Enterprise Architecture becomes a decision engine, not static documentation.

## Core Pillars

- **One integrated toolchain** across the transformation lifecycle (not point tools)
- **Shared source of truth** that aligns Business + IT
- **Outcome-led transformation:** identify value, prioritize what matters, execute with confidence
- **Digital twin mindset** to predict, manage, and continuously improve change

## Elevator Pitch

### 30 Seconds
ERP transformation is hard because reality is fragmented — across people, process, applications, and data. The integrated toolchain unifies those signals into one source of truth, so you can identify value, choose the right path, de-risk execution, and keep improving after go-live.

### 90 Seconds
Most transformation programs slow down because teams don't share the same facts: change impacts are unclear, process reality is hidden, application dependencies are opaque, and adoption risk is underestimated.

The integrated toolchain connects People, Processes, Applications, and Data into a unified transformation view — so Business and IT can align on priorities, quantify value, plan and execute change with confidence, and continuously innovate after go-live.

Most organizations do not fully track value realization. ERP transformation requires active value management, not static KPIs. SAP Signavio enables continuous value identification, tracking, and realization — linking process insight directly to financial and operational outcomes.

## When to Engage

### ✅ Green Lights
- Planning or budgeting ERP transformation (ECC → S/4HANA and/or RISE with SAP)
- Need to reduce cost, risk, and effort in transformation programs
- Documentation is low quality, current-state transparency is missing
- Want to quantify benefits and compare multiple scenarios (e.g., multiple ECC instances)
- Need to assess process impact — even for lift-and-shift — to avoid surprises
- Projects delayed, need a reset with fact-based insights
- Want continuous value realization post go-live
- ERP transformation success defined by outcomes, not just go-live
- Struggle to connect process, application, and data decisions

### 🛑 Red Lights
- Only asking for a single point solution without transformation scope
- No transformation trigger, no change initiative, no appetite to act

## Discovery Questions

1. What transformation decision do you need to make in the next 90 days — and what information is missing today?
2. Where are your biggest risks: people adoption, process reality, application dependencies, or data readiness?
3. How many ERP instances / process variants exist today — and where is complexity slowing you down?
4. How do you currently quantify value and prioritize change initiatives across business and IT?
5. What would "success" look like 6 months after go-live — beyond technical cutover?
6. Where do architecture decisions slow down transformation today?
7. How do you understand application cost, risk, and dependencies today?
8. How do you govern AI usage and AI agents across the enterprise?
9. What decisions would be easier if architecture, process, and value were connected?
10. How do you currently track value realization across your ERP transformation?
11. Which transformation initiatives are tied to measurable outcomes today?
12. Where do value hypotheses get lost between planning and execution?
