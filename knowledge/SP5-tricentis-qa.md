## Meaning

Enable continuous quality for SAP-centric enterprise landscapes by reducing risk and accelerating innovation through an integrated approach to quality assurance across SAP and non-SAP applications — so customers can deliver change with confidence and protect business continuity.

## Core Pillars

- Move from "testing as a phase" to **continuous quality** embedded in the lifecycle
- Reduce the risk of change by **knowing what's impacted** and prioritizing what to test
- Improve speed and reliability of releases across complex SAP + non-SAP environments

## Elevator Pitch

### 30 Seconds
SAP landscapes change constantly — transports, support packs, upgrades, and migrations. The risk isn't change itself; it's not knowing what's impacted and not validating the right things fast enough. Tricentis helps you assess impact, test continuously, and assure performance — so you accelerate innovation with less risk.

### 90 Seconds
Structured as **Assess → Test → Assure.** Start by assessing change impact so you can prioritize risk and focus on what matters. Then automate continuous testing of business-critical processes across SAP and surrounding technologies. Finally, assure performance and stability at scale so releases meet expected user experience and SLAs — reducing defects and hypercare while improving release speed.

## When to Engage

### ✅ Green Lights
- Too much change and too much risk: upgrades, support packs, transports, migrations
- Need to accelerate innovation without breaking production
- Don't know what's impacted by changes; hypercare is too high
- Need end-to-end test coverage across SAP plus surrounding technologies
- Want to simulate external interfaces / APIs during testing to reduce cost and dependency
- Pursuing clean core strategy with business-critical custom extensions that must stay reliable
- Considering replacing an incumbent testing vendor

### 🛑 Red Lights
- Customer only looking for process improvement without QA risk/speed challenges (→ SP2)
- Customer only wants EA transparency without a testing/quality outcome (→ SP3)

## Discovery Questions

1. What types of change create the most risk — transports, support packs, upgrades, or migrations?
2. How do you decide what to test today — and how often do you discover impact too late?
3. What is your current hypercare / production defect pattern after releases?
4. How broad is your landscape beyond SAP — how many technologies must be covered end-to-end?
5. Do you need to simulate APIs / interfaces during testing to reduce external dependencies and cost?
6. How does your clean core strategy handle validation of business-critical custom extensions?

### Assure
Validate performance, stability, and experience at scale.
- Ensure readiness for go-live with performance evidence
- Meet SLAs and user experience expectations
